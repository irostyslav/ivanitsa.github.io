Welcome

 

The majority of IT organizations I know try to base their IT strategy and priorities on the business strategy and needs. Once these strategic imperatives are known and well understood we build our IT plans and define our programs to deliver against those priorities. The first problem encountered is if business and IT are asked about their corporate strategy only half of the people involved agree they have a shared understanding. The second problem is causing even more trouble in our daily lives: the gap between Strategy and Execution. How can we build sound plans based on our business strategy if execution fails? (I will not go into detail into the third problem that less than 15% of the companies compare their results against the defined strategic plans).
